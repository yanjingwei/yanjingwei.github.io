Default Content in WordPress

Two primary types of default content, pages and posts.

Pages in WordPress are what are referred to as static content, meaning that the content generally lives in one specific place on the site with its own URL.
Pages can be edited and updated, but they are not generally changed too much once they are created. Pages are hierarchical, meaning that they can have a parent, child relationship where one main page has several sub-pages. This relationship is often reflected in drop down menus.

Posts on the other hand, are what are referred to as dynamic content, meaning that they are published regularly and often appear on different places throughout a site.

Posts can be grouped using categories and tags, which help identify common topics and details that connect or identify posts.
